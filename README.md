# Affective Human-Robot Mutual Learning in Socially Assistive Robotics

### MSc Research Dissertation — University of Hertfordshire (2025/26)

## Overview
A critical review of Differential Outcomes Training (DOT) applied 
to Socially Assistive Robotics (SAR). This dissertation evaluates 
adaptive emotional learning frameworks that enable robots to detect, 
interpret, and respond to human emotional cues in real-time 
assistive care environments.

## Research Focus
- Affective computing in human-robot interaction (HRI)
- Reinforcement learning frameworks for emotional adaptation
- Differential Outcomes Training (DOT) methodology
- Applications in eldercare and assistive robotics

## Key Contributions
- Critical review of existing DOT frameworks in SAR
- Evaluation of affective computing approaches for HRI
- Analysis of differential outcomes in robot learning
- Recommendations for emotionally intelligent robot design
